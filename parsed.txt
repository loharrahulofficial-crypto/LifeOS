How to Execute -- Inside LifeOS
Six concrete steps to run this mission using FRIDAY in your LifeOS app. These are physical actions in order -- no theory,
just what you do.
Step 1 -- Tell FRIDAY about
the mission
Open FRIDAY in your LifeOS app and say: 'FRIDAY, I'm starting Operation Zero-G --
a personal antigravity research project. I want you to track my progress, assign me
daily tasks, and tell me which AI model to use for each problem I bring you.' FRIDAY
will store this as a mission fact in her Core Files and update her system prompt
permanently.

habits
FRIDAY will suggest adding Phase 1 habits to LifeOS: Physics study (1hr daily), Arxiv

Step 3 -- Get your morning
brief
Say 'FRIDAY, morning brief' each day. She checks your LifeOS data -- habits
completed, tasks due, focus sessions -- and gives you a 60-second mission status on
what Phase 1 needs from you today. She queues 3 tasks in your LifeOS task manager
automatically.
Step 4 -- Bring FRIDAY your
blockers
When you hit a physics problem you can't solve, describe it to FRIDAY. She
diagnoses the problem type, decides which model to route it to based on the routing
matrix, crafts the optimal prompt for that model, and returns a synthesis. She cites
which model generated what.
Step 5 -- Weekly mission
review
Every Sunday, say 'FRIDAY, weekly mission review.' She pulls your habit streak data,
completed tasks, and any breakthroughs from the week. She updates the mission
timeline, flags risks to the current phase exit milestone, and tells you if you're on track.
She self-updates her memory with anything new.
Step 6 -- Phase advancementWhen you hit a phase milestone, tell FRIDAY: 'FRIDAY, Operation Zero-G Phase N
exit milestone achieved.' She formally closes that phase in her Core Files, unlocks the
next phase task stack, switches her default AI model routing to the next phase's model
stack, and generates your new habit set.
